III.

The Loop.

The Socratic round is not a fixed-iteration loop. It is convergence-driven. Three signals decide whether to keep going or stop.

Three signals. One judgment call.

The orchestrator does not stop on a counter; it stops when the reasoning is solid. Convergence is informed by signal — not formula.

σ

Probability stability

Top-5 max shift < 0.05 across rounds suggests settling. A large shift driven by genuinely new evidence is healthy, not a reason to keep looping.

¬

Unresolved violations

If the Challenger flagged a critical constraint violation on a top hypothesis and the Diagnostician hasn't addressed or conceded it, keep going.

∆

Information gain

Each round must produce at least one hypothesis or mechanistic connection not present in the prior round. If a round adds nothing, stop. Don't iterate for iteration's sake.

IV.

Anti-anchoring is structural.

The 20 anti-anchoring constraints don't live in one big checklist. They are propagated to each agent at the depth that agent can act on — prevention, verification, integration, final review.

V.

Twelve principles, three picked.

The skill carries twelve key principles. These three describe its philosophy of work better than the other nine.
i.

You are smarter than the pipeline.

The orchestrator adapts the workflow as findings emerge. Phases skip, repeat, or reorder when the evidence demands it. The score is canonical, not rigid.

ii.

Panel disagreements are diagnostic gold.

Push on them. Don't smooth them. The reconciliation that papers over a real disagreement is hiding the most informative signal the orchestra produced.

iii.

Information gain matters.

Each round must produce new insights — a connection, a probability shift, a previously unidentified gap. If a round adds nothing, the orchestra is restating, not deliberating. Stop.
